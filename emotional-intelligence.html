Intelligence Quotient vs Emotional Quotient

Intelligence Quotient (IQ) is a measurement of a person's cognitive abilities to problem-solve, reason and process information. It is usually assessed using standardised tests to evaluate skills. Essentially the higher the IQ means higher analytical thinking, quicker learning and the ability to apply the knowledge.

Emotional Quotient (EQ) is how well a person can 'read the room', that is to recognise, understand and manage emotions of their own and others. Skills such as empathy, self-awareness, self-regulation and social skills are key in doing so.

IQ can give you an advantage in technical and analytical work, but EQ can be the driving factor in long-term success. EQ is important for leadership, teamwork, conflict resolution and personal well-being.

Analogy: In a workplace IQ is to know what to say, whereas EQ is to know how and when say it so it is well received
