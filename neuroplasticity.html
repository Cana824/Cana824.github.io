Research Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

Neuroplasticity is important for people to understand. They would be able to innately understand the fact that things are difficult solely because you have YET to fully understand the topic. New synapses are being built as an individual attempts to learn something new.
In the health care system, neuroplasticity is important particularly for stroke patients. Whilst having damaged neurons, neuroplasticity enables them to rewire and create new neural pathways to recover lost functions. This allows them to improve their quality of life. Thus, understanding the principles of neuroplasticity is beneficial, as it can be applied in many aspects of learning.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit

Personally, I will apply neuroplasticity alongside each sprint. By knowing that learning is a process and that improvement and understanding is a curve. I should challenge myself with what I do not understand to create the new neuron pathways to develop myself. Utilising the acronym from the psychologist Carol Dewick - A.G.E.S, I can ensure Attention is adequate whilst tackling sprints, Generation by associating various known knowledge to what I am currently learning, E by adding in how I feel in the moment, and Spacing new knowledge apart so I have sufficient time to process knowledge and also have several opportunities to return the topic.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

From a medical understanding neuroplasticity - things such a challenging self with new things like music or skills, decreasing stress, regular exercise, and quality sleep all can increase neuroplasticity.
For myself, I believe that I need to ensure that I reduce my stressors and sleep an appropriate number of hours to boost my neuroplasticity. According to M. Puderbaugh ad P. Emmady 2023, “reducing stress and avoiding sleep deprivation have been shown to be helpful to improve memory, attention span, and other domains of cognition”. I am happy about my level of physical activity and the DevAcademy course is a huge challenge.

Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

Growth mindset is the belief that growth can be developed through applying self and dedication. Growth mindset is relevant in DevAcademy and in life as it embraces the fact that mistakes and flaws are not dead ends, it is an opportunity moment to learn. Challenges are positive situations where new things can be realised. Thus, to succeed in study and in life, a growth mindset is beneficial.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

Whilst exploring neuroplasticity and growth mindsets, I was surprised that whilst I actively said things such as “I love challenges”, I will also quickly say things like “this is too hard”. I firmly believe that by resetting myself in the situation of wanting to give up, I would be able to improve myself at a better rate. I will aspire to change myself to not give up and try for 5 extra minutes when hitting hurdles whilst still knowing when to ask for help.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

To integrate growth mindset into your learning journey, I will actively seek challenges and apply mindfulness and add 'YET' for when I am struggling. I aim to have more patience with myself and show more kindness in the journey.

Blend with your learning plan and strategies

Reflect and discuss how these understandings may shape or influence your learning plan and strategy.

Understanding neuroplasticity and growth mindset has given me a clearer direction for shaping my learning plan. Knowing that my brain can form new pathways helps me reframe challenges as part of the learning process rather than as obstacles. This means I will intentionally design my study strategy around practice, repetition, and persistence, allowing myself to sit with discomfort when something feels difficult, because that is when growth is happening.

At the same time, adopting a growth mindset will help me manage setbacks more productively. Instead of seeing mistakes as failures, I will treat them as feedback loops- pointing me toward what I need to strengthen understanding of. To put this into practice, I will add the word “yet” to moments of struggle (e.g., “I don't understand this yet”), which will help me stay motivated and maintain patience with myself.

Together, these principles will shape my learning plan by:

  • • Building in spaced repetition to strengthen memory (neuroplasticity principle).
  • • Creating a routine that balances focus, rest, and reflection, so I can process and integrate new knowledge.
  • • Actively seeking challenging tasks rather than avoiding them, using them as opportunities for growth.
  • • Practising self-kindness and resilience when I hit hurdles, leaning on the growth mindset to persist.

Ultimately, these understandings encourage me to view my learning journey as dynamic and evolving. With neuroplasticity, I know my brain can adapt; with a growth mindset, I know I can guide that adaptation through effort and perseverance. This combined approach gives me a stronger, more sustainable strategy for progressing in DevAcademy and beyond.
