BootCamp Learning Plan

My Bootcamp Learning Plan

As I progress from Foundations into Bootcamp, I have reflected on my goals, strenths and challenges that I will face. I write this learning plan as a commitment to how I will approach the next stage of my DevAcademy learning and how I will see myself grow technically and personally.

My long-term goals

My long term goal is to transition out of the healthcare field and into the IT industry. This will be a significant shift from my last 8 years as a Medical Laboratory Scientist, but is what I look forward to. IT interests me as it constantly evolves, there are many opportunities to problem solve and allows me to continuously learn throughout my career.

When I graduate from DevAcademy, I hope to transition into a developer role where I can apply both analytical skills from my Science background and new technical knowledge I can get with DevAcademy. Beyond just getting a job, I want to show others that a big career change is possible with dedication.

Strengths and Limitations

One of my greatest strengths is my curiosity. I enjoy being very hands on with something that interests me. When the initial spark of curiosity fades, I have the discipline and maturity to continue moving forward. I am also analytical and am able to prioritise effectively which comes with the maturity I have gained in my early 30s.

My limitations are also important to recognise. Balancing part-time study with full-time shift work is a big challenge, and I find myself holding myself (and others) to very high expectations, which can be stressful. Through Foundations, I have also realised that I can work things out on my own (with a lot of googling). While persistence is useful, it can also slow me down due to my own stubbornes.

These strengths and limitations will shape my Bootcamp experience. My curiousity and adaptability will help me embrance challenges, which my high standards and hesitation to ask for help are things I need to manage carefully.

Non-Technical Challenges ahead

I am expecting my biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be working in groups and learning to trust others. Past experiences have made me wary of relying too heavily on the team, and I know that collaboration is a key element of Bootcamp. Thus, letting go of perfectionism and embracing different ways of working will be essential for me.

I also anticipate that the intensity of the next 12 weeks will test my energy and motivation. I know that staying consistent, pacing myself and maintaining balance will be just as important as writing code.

Human SKills I want to develop

During DevAcademy, I want to grow in the areaas of communication, teamwork, and emotional intelligence. I want to learn how to give and receive constructive feedback, improve how I collaborate with different personalities, and develop my confidenc ein speaking up and sharing ideas. I also want to work on my resilience and self-awareness so I can manage my expectations of myself and others more effectively.

These skills will support my throughout Bootcamp and will help me work better as a team player.

Expectations of the DevAcademy Team and Myself

I still expect guidance, encouragement, and feedback from the DevAcademyteam to help me find my own answers than to give answers directly. For me, support looks like approachable facilitators, who are open to questions and a safe environment where it is okay to make mistakes.

From myself, I expect commitment and perseverance. I will proactively manage my time, and be repectful and honest with challenges and how I work in a group. I will ensure that I contribute and make sure I ask questions and help when I need to.

Seeking Help and Managing workload

To avoid staying stuck for too long, I will look over my code systematically, check for resources to guide me, reach out to others, facilitators or my partner. Additionally, when I find myself too frustrated to progress and am not learning, I will take a short break to give me the clarity I need.

To manage my workload, I will use the timeboxes as a guide, plan my shift schedule in advance, and communicate early if I start to feel overwhelmed. I will take care of my wellbeing - physical and mental so I can show up fully. I will work productively and respectfullywith my peers, facilitators by being clear, kind and professional.

In summary: Bootcamp will be a challenge. It is not solely about learning code, but also about building resilience, trust, and collaboration. My goal is to successfully graduate with the technical and human skills required to become a good developer and beyond.
