1. I don't know myself as much as I thought.

2. I learnt that I doubt myself more than I realise, especially in situations where I feel unprepared. My ego gets in the way of asking for help. Thus, now I've realised that it is important to be more vulnerable to strengthen learning.

3. EQ and IQ are just as important as one another. They come hand in hand in creating the person you are.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

My values help keep me grounded in how I work with other people. Empathy enables me to collaborate effectively with other people. In programming, it is an essential skill to have as it requires a lot of collaborative problem solving. Self-awareness will allow me to recognise my own limitations and ask for support when I need it - all essential in learning and professional development.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

I was surprised at the amount of self-reflection human skills learning involved. It is helping me become more self-aware, improving my communication and impacting how I approach working with others.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

THe most challenging part was being open and vulnerable. Acknowledging my flaws and ego is eye opening. Another challenge is slowing down and deeply reflecting. This is because it was an aspect that I did not realise how much I didn't know about myself.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I believe that it is an imporatnt skillset to have. An individual who is able to have these skills are likely more capable of open communication, being more vulnerable and open to feedback, thus, are better team players.
